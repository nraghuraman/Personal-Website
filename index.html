about me

My number one passion is constant learning, and this underscores everything I do. From my free time to the student groups I’m a part of, I strive to treat every situation as a chance to grow.

As a student at Stanford University majoring in Computer Science, I am pushing myself to learn as much about engineering as possible. I am a member of Stanford Student Space Initiative’s Cycloon team, which is developing helium balloons which may eventually revolutionize hurricane data collection. Thus far, I have developed a helium vent for the apex of a Cycloon balloon, which is vital for the system's long-distance flight. Additionally, I have worked on the programming of flight avionics using Arduino. I am also looking forward to beginning as a CS 106A section leader in the autumn as a part of Stanford's CS 198 program. In this role, I will teach a section of 8-12 students as a part of Stanford's introductory computer science sequence.

Moreover, I am looking forward to using my school breaks as an opportunity to delve further into the fields that excite me. This summer, I am working at Software Engineering Professionals in Carmel, IN as a Software Engineering Intern. Simultaneously, I am working on a software project as an Innovation Fellow with NeuroLex Labs. I am thrilled to have these opportunities to learn more about the field of software engineering and grow my skill set.

In my free time, I enjoy studying linguistics, watching YouTube videos on any and every topic, and designing and folding my own origami models.

Thank you for visiting my website!
